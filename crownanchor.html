Crown and Anchor

Welcome

Crown and Anchor is a betting game originally played by Royal Navy midshipmen in the 18th century. A mat with six squares was laid out, with each square marked with a different symbol: Crown, Anchor, and the four standard card suits. (See photo on right.) Players would bet by placing money on one or more of the squares, one of the players would roll 3 dice whose faces matched the 6 symbols on the mat, and players who placed bets on squares which matched the rolled die faces won.

Similar versions of this game have appeared in other cultures, and the game is still played today in some casinos and gatherings like fairs and church fundraisers.

For this online version of the game, we will use the following rules:

  • You will start with a Royal Navy midshipman's weekly pay of 100 pence for your kitty.
  • For each round, you can bet on one or more of the 6 squares, as long as your total bet for all the squares doesn't exceed your kitty.
  • You will click a Roll Dice button which will randomly generate results simulating rolling 3 six-sided dice.
  • If a die face matches one of the squares on which you placed your bet, you win your bet (you take your original bet plus an equal amount from the house). If 2 or 3 faces match a square on which you placed a bet, you win back your original bet plus 2 or 3 times that amount from the house.
  • You lose any bets you placed on squares which do not match rolled die faces.
  • Your net winnings or losses for the round increase or decrease your kitty for the next round.
  • You win the game if you make an additional 100 pence on top of what you started with.
  • You lose the game if your kitty reaches 0.
  • You can also quit the game at any time.
